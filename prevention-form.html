How to measure your waist:
1. Stand upright and breathe normally.
2. Find the midpoint between your lowest rib and the top of your hip bone — roughly level with your belly button.
3. Wrap a tape measure around your bare skin at this point. It should sit flat without pressing in.
4. Take the reading at the end of a gentle exhale.

24-Hour Blood Pressure Monitor (ABPM)

Consent and device responsibility

What is an ABPM? The ABPM is a small device worn on your upper arm for 24 hours. It automatically checks your blood pressure every 30 minutes during the day and every hour at night, giving a much more accurate picture than a single surgery reading.

Your nurse may recommend an ABPM at your appointment based on your blood pressure reading and overall cardiovascular risk profile. If you are unsure whether this applies to you, your nurse will advise you on the day.
Please make a selection regarding the ABPM.

ABPM Care Instructions — Please Read

  • Keep dry — no baths, showers, or swimming while wearing the monitor. A sponge bath is fine.
  • During readings — the cuff inflates every 30 minutes during the day and every hour overnight. Keep your arm still and relaxed at your side until each reading is complete.
  • Clothing — wear loose-fitting clothing that allows the cuff to sit freely on your upper arm for the full 24 hours.
  • Exercise — avoid heavy or vigorous exercise during the monitoring period.
  • Sleep — the monitor will take readings overnight. This is normal and expected.
  • Discomfort — you may adjust the cuff slightly if it becomes uncomfortable. Do not remove the device unless you are in severe discomfort. Contact the practice if needed: (01) 845 2500.
  • Return — return the device to Kinsealy Medical Centre the following morning, or at the time agreed with your nurse. Failure to return the device will result in a replacement charge.
  • Damage or loss — you are responsible for the device while it is in your care. Any damage or loss will be assessed by the practice and a replacement charge may apply.

What to Expect on the Day

Your appointment at a glance

🛡️ Your Prevention Programme Appointment
1
Arrive fasting — no food or drink (except water) from midnight the night before. Continue all regular medications as normal.
2
Check in at reception — let staff know you are here for your Prevention Programme review.
3
Nurse / Phlebotomist — will take blood samples (cholesterol, glucose, and other relevant tests), check your blood pressure, weight, and height, and fit a 24-hour blood pressure monitor (ABPM) if required. This may take 20–30 minutes.
4
Doctor review — your GP will review your results and discuss any lifestyle or medication recommendations. If follow-up is needed, they will contact you.
5
Your script — a 12-month prescription will be issued. A note is sent to your pharmacy to remind you to book your next review when the script is running out.
