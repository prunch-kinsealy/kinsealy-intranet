Corticosteroid Joint Injection

Please read all sections carefully before signing. This form confirms your informed consent to receive a corticosteroid joint injection at Kinsealy Medical Centre. If you have any questions, please contact us before completing this form.

What is it?

A corticosteroid (steroid) and local anaesthetic mixture is injected directly into or around the affected joint or soft tissue to reduce inflammation and relieve pain.

Expected benefit

Pain relief and improved range of movement, typically starting within 3–7 days and lasting up to 3–6 months. Results vary between patients.

On the day

  • Wear loose clothing to expose the area
  • Bring your steroid script if you are a GMS patient
  • Bring your insurance details if applicable
  • Allow 30 minutes for the appointment
  • You may drive afterwards unless advised otherwise

Aftercare

  • Rest the joint for 24–48 hours
  • Avoid strenuous activity for 2 weeks
  • Ice pack for post-injection flare (first 24–48 hrs)
  • Keep the site dry for 24 hours
  • Contact us if redness, warmth, or fever develops

Risks & Informed Consent

Risks of Corticosteroid Joint Injection

Post-injection flare — temporary increase in pain and swelling at the injection site in the first 24–48 hours. This is common (up to 10% of patients) and settles with rest, ice, and analgesia.

Infection (septic arthritis) — very rare (<1 in 10,000). Signs include significant worsening of pain, heat, swelling, or fever after 48 hours. Seek urgent medical review if this occurs.

Skin changes — depigmentation (lightening of skin) or fat atrophy (small dimple) at the injection site. More common with repeated injections or superficial placements.

Tendon weakening — repeated injections into or near a tendon may weaken it over time. For this reason, injections are limited to a maximum of 3 per joint per year.

Blood glucose rise — patients with diabetes may experience a temporary rise in blood sugar for 24–72 hours following the injection. Increased monitoring is advised during this period.

Facial flushing — a brief sensation of warmth or redness in the face may occur within 24–48 hours. This is harmless and self-limiting.

Bleeding / bruising — especially in patients taking anticoagulants. Firm pressure will be applied after the injection.

No guarantee of benefit — the injection does not work for everyone. If there is no improvement after 4 weeks, further assessment will be arranged.
