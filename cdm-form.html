Chronic Disease Management
Pre-Appointment Health Update

Please complete this form before your CDM or Prevention Programme appointment. It helps your nurse and doctor prepare and makes the most of your time at the surgery. The form takes about 5 minutes.

🍽️

Fasting Required — Please Read Before Your Appointment

You must fast from midnight the night before your appointment. This means no food or drink except plain water from midnight onwards. Fasting is needed for accurate blood results (glucose, cholesterol, triglycerides).

Medications: Take all regular medications as normal, with a small sip of water — except as noted below.

⚠️

Blood Pressure Medication — Morning Appointments Only

If you take a blood pressure medication ending in -pril (e.g. Ramipril, Lisinopril, Perindopril, Enalapril) or -sartan (e.g. Losartan, Candesartan, Valsartan, Irbesartan), your appointment must be in the morning.

If you are unsure whether this applies to you, please ring the practice before your appointment. Do not take these tablets on the morning of an afternoon appointment.

What to Expect on the Day

Your appointment at a glance

🏥 Your CDM / PP Appointment
1
Arrive fasting — no food or drink (except water) from midnight the night before. Continue all medications as normal unless you take an ACE inhibitor or ARB and have an afternoon appointment (see the orange box above).
2
Check in at reception — let staff know you are here for your CDM or Prevention Programme review.
3
Nurse / Phlebotomist — will take blood samples, check your blood pressure, weight, and height, and place a 24-hour blood pressure monitor (ABPM) if required. This may take 20–30 minutes.
4
Doctor review — your GP will review your results and renew your chronic disease prescription. If follow-up is needed, they will contact you.
5
Your script — a 6-month prescription (CDM) or 12-month prescription (PP) will be issued. A note is sent to your pharmacy to remind you to book your next review when the script is running out.
6
Bring with you — medication list or tablets, glasses if needed, and your ABPM monitor if you already have one fitted.
