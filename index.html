Chronic Disease Management (CDM / PP / OCF)

▼
👤 Created by: Dr F. O'Reilly 🚧 Under Construction 🔄 Last updated: 23/05/2025 📌 v1.0
MS
Step 1 — Medical Secretary / All Staff
Book the Patient
Preferred: Calendly link (text/email from Socrates) — Calendly Link. Cancel a slot at time of booking.
PP (annual) bookable from 9–10 months. CDM (bi-annual) bookable from 4–5 months.
Patient must complete GDrive 'CDM Patient Information' form — in-waiting room option available.
Book with: Nurse OR Phlebotomist + GP (if CDM). PP does not require GP visit unless Phlebotomist + CDM.
❌ No afternoon bookings for patients on ACE inhibitors (ending in 'pril') or ARBs (ending in 'sartan'). If unsure — ask a GP.
NS
Step 2 — Nurse / Phlebotomist
Surgery Visit
  • Ensure CDM Patient Information form is uploaded before calling patient
  • Use 'CDM Note' template in Socrates
  • Update Diagnoses → Register any chronic condition
  • Use AliveCor — if not NSR, arrange full ECG. Place ABPM
  • Complete CDM bloods as per note template (other bloods are chargeable)
  • For OCF, task nurse for PP follow-up if eligible
  • Populate all into Socrates CDM tab. Submit STC (ECG & ABPM) via Socrates
  • 🟠 Task doctor: "CDM doctor review and 6/12 script" — include bloods + ABPM under same doctor name. Distribute evenly (Orange priority)
DR
Step 3 — Doctor
GP Review & Script
Review: Bloods, ABPM, AliveCor/ECG, chart notes.
Decide if follow-up needed (call, visit, text).
Do 6/12 (CDM) or 12/12 (PP) script. Include note to pharmacist:
"Please advise patient to book CDM review when script finishes"
Remind patient: next script requires CDM review. Rebook when last script is issued — do not order script, book CDM/PP.
PM
Step 4 — Practice Manager
Submission (Weekly)
  • Confirm CDM/PP submission. Confirm STC submitted (ECG, ABPM)
  • Document in chart: "CDM/PP submitted"
  • Review failed submissions every 1–2 weeks
  • Bulk text reminders every 3 months (Google Calendar reminder set)
ALL
Opportunistic — All Staff
OCF — Opportunistic Case Finding
See a GMS/DVC patient? Check if CDM/PP/OCF is due! Add CDM/PP as Active Diagnosis in chart.
If script requested, check last CDM visit. No recent CDM → give 1/12 script only and send CDM text template.
Eligibility: CDM (8 HSE conditions). PP if Qrisk >20%, Pre-diabetic, or Hypertension + GMS/DVC + Age >45. Any woman with Gestational DM or Pre-Eclampsia since 01/2023.
OCF = first step, then PP or CDM. Claim both!

Panel Management (PCRS Importer)

▼
👤 PM / Dr F. O'Reilly🚧 Under Construction🔄 Last updated: 23/05/2025
⚠️ Two critical times: 1st of every month and middle of every month. Use Socrates manual "PCRS importer".

D) Over-70s: Every 3 months, search for over-70s with no medical card and send registration link text.

Late Arrivals Policy

▼
👤 Dr F. O'Reilly🚧 Under Construction🔄 Last updated: 23/05/2025
⏱️ The lateness clock starts from when the patient is checked in by reception (not from their physical arrival time).
0–5 mins late: Check in as normal.
10 mins late: Inform patient they are late. Contact GP to confirm if they can still be seen. If accommodated → one urgent issue only.
20 mins late: Appointment considered missed — must rebook.
20+ mins: Strictly missed. No exceptions (see below).

Exceptions:
Sick babies/children: do not turn away — squeeze into current session, advise wait.
Elderly/vulnerable: exercise patience, facilitate where possible.
Internal: if original GP can't see, check if another doctor has a free slot.

When patients complain doctors run late:
"We always endeavour to run to time. However, when appointments start late, it creates a knock-on effect for every subsequent patient. To ensure every patient gets their full allocated time, we ask everyone arrives promptly."

▼
👤 Dr F. O'Reilly🚧 Under Construction🔄 Last updated: 23/05/2025

3rd DNA (within 18 months): Notify PM immediately. PM + Partner review. Decision documented. If removal agreed → letter to patient.

Check booked list against attendance. Mark any DNA in chart with stage (1st, 2nd, 3rd). Send appropriate text. Escalate 3rd DNA to PM immediately.

Fatty Liver / Deranged LFTs (NAFLD/MASLD)

▼
👤 Dr F. O'Reilly🚧 Under Construction🔄 Last updated: 22/04/2026
📖 NICE NG49 — Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease ↗ | FIB-4: MDCalc ↗ | Patient info: tinyurl.com/yc2nfxu3 ↗
DR
Doctor — Deranged LFTs Found
Initial Work-Up
Order liver ultrasound + full LFT screen, hepatitis serology, ferritin/iron, TFT, fasting glucose, lipids, BMI. Exclude: alcohol, medications, viral hepatitis, haemochromatosis, autoimmune hepatitis.
→ Text: "Abnormal LFTs — Refer Ultrasound"
DR
Doctor — Ultrasound Result + FIB-4
FIB-4 Score & Decision
FIB-4 = (Age × AST) ÷ (Platelets × √ALT)
<1.30 = Low risk → reassure, lifestyle, annual LFTs → Text: "Fatty Liver — New (Low/Intermediate FIB-4)"
1.30–2.67 = Intermediate → repeat 1–2 yrs, optimise lifestyle
≥1.45/≥2.67 = High risk → refer hepatology Fibroscan → Text: "Fatty Liver — FIB-4 >1.45, Refer Fibroscan"
Lifestyle: weight loss 5–10%, reduce alcohol, Mediterranean diet.

Confirm raised calcium on repeat. Order: PTH (paired), Vit D, phosphate, U&E, 24hr urine calcium, ALP.
